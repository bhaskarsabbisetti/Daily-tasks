Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist, educator, and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). Known as the Missile Man of India, he played a key role in India's space and missile programs, contributing to the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. He was also a major force behind India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. Beyond his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam was a passionate educator and motivator. He traveled extensively to inspire students and young minds. His vision for India’s development is outlined in "Vision 2020", where he emphasized technology, education, and economic growth. He was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.
